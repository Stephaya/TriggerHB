This Tabletop Homebrew ruleset is inspired by Tactical Games and made to fit within the TableTop Gameplay Format. This Homebrew is made to be easy for novices; however, it leaves just enough complexity for higher level players to utilize and enjoy the unique combat system present. This Homebrew gives players more creative freedom to act. Many new Mechanics introduce new Opportunities for the Players to Use.

Battles are Dynamic. Battles are Fast. Battles are Concentrated.

â–ˆ Inside of Social Gameplay

Tabletop Roleplay has Gameplay inside and outside of the Social Aspect. The Idea of Tabletop Roleplays is to Expand your comfort zone and Act and Talk more openly with others. The Social Aspect here is Part of the Bread and Butter of All Tabletop Games. If no one Acts, the Game will become Stale.

â–ˆ ADHD Roleplay

The ADHD Roleplay Style in this Homebrew Popularizes a Lack of Maps with a Heavy Emphasis on Interacting through the world. This Interacting happens through the Game Master, as player explore the world through Directional Means, with Visuals Relating to the Area. Sometimes before ADHD Roleplay can begin, the Game Master May Ask Everyone to Roll D20s to See What Type of Events could happen to them. 

The Fundamental Process that ADHD roleplay takes is through processes similar to Point and Click Adventures. Characters are taken from One Location to Another with simple connections in the World. Characters and Interesting Items are more concentrated this way, allowing more engaging and Focused Gameplay.

Characters Take Turns acting within Areas. If Multiple Characters are within the same Area, they all share the same Turn. All Characters' Turns Function off of their Initiative; Characters with the Highest Initiative can act First. Once a Character Moves to a new Area, their Turn ends, and the Character with the next Initiative can Act in their Area. When Characters move into a new Area or area- they should be told details about the area.

Combat can Initiate in Roleplay, However, Characters not within the Area are not drawn into Combat. Combat is only Initiated through natrual agreement, preemptive strikes, or directed strikes. All Characters in the Same Area are drawn into combat. Characters Are given Two Turns in Combat before Combat Pauses and the next Character may act in their Area. Characters Outside of Combat can Affect Combat Zones through Outside Forces. However, Interacting with the Combat Zone from a far distance will instantly end their turn. Interacting with Combat Zones will function through Combat Zone Rules (@adhd_combat).

â–ˆ Reputation

Reputation in this Homebrew Functions as current Social Status of the Party. Reputation directly ties to how other Characters will Treat the Player Characters. Reputation Works on Scale from -50 to 50. Reputation could start anywhere, but the Scale dictates how Characters are Treated. Reputation increases through Interactions with other Characters, Impressions, and Questlines. Lower Reputation Scores Typically Means Characters are Feared and Hated- While being Well-known with those of Low Reputation. Higher Reputation Typically Means Characters are Adored and Respected- While being Hated or Targeted with those of High Reputation. Reputation is not related to Character's Alignments; Characters Can be Evil and still hold High Reputation. 

Reputation Can Enable Characters to Have Higher Relations with Characters. Reputation can also allow Access to Questlines or Exclusive Content. Reputation Functions as the Non-Linear Key the Players Mold during their Adventures.

â–ˆ Relationships

Relationships in this Homebrew Functions as Personal Social Links between Characters. Relationships help develop characters with each other. Players can Increase or Decrease these Relationships to get personal details about each other or benefits. Bonds can also be formed to create allies that could eventually join the Players Party (If the Game Master Allows It). Lower Relationships Typically Means that Characters are Rivals or Enemies of Eachother. Higher Relationships Typically Means that Characters are Friends or Lovers of Eachother. This means that these Relationships Can help With Questlines in Connecting with Certain Characters or Using Certain Characters, Or Relationships can provide Character Development for Them.

Companions Modifications are Possibly the Most Direct Reward Relationships Give as they Provide Buffs and Modifiers for Befriending Other Characters. Relationships Function as the Tools the Players use to Further the Game and their Characters. 

For more Details on Character Relationships and the Effects they have, see (@player_creation-character_relationships).

â–ˆ Player Contract

The purpose of this contract is to lay ground roles and procedures for both the Players and the Game Master to ensure a cool and good gaming experience, all players will be required to agree to this Player's Contract if they are to be allowed to continue playing the game. Breach of Contract will result in verbal warnings and repeated offenses CAN result in ejection from the game if issues continue. Player Contracts are followed by both the Game Master and Players; Unless the Player Contract is not Used. Player Contracts are determined based on the Game Master's Own Decision.
